Programmer

It all started when I was a bored eight-year-old kid wanting to make computer malware to annoy family members. I learned batch programming, a little bit of web design here, some C there and eventually a four-semester course on Java thanks to school. Despite having learnt quite a handful of languages over the years, I proudly choose Go as my weapon of choice since it is faster than Python, and more productive than C, therefore possessing a balanced profile.

Hacker

Even more than creating technology, I feel a greater sense of joy in ripping technology apart and taking a look inside. My first read on hacking, "Hacking: The Art of Exploitation" by Jon Erickson, was probably one of the most influencing books I have read till date. By the age of ten, I switched over to linux (albeit, Ubuntu) since I found it more flexible for programming and writing tools and exploits. Three years later, I stumbled upon Kali by Offensive Security and stuck with it for the next two years.

Open-Sourcerer

Although the switching to Ubuntu and later Kali from Windows was very educational, the final distro-hop came in the form of (you guessed it) Arch Linux. Since then, I stopped distro-hopping and started creating tools and custom exploits for different services. Having been in the Linux realm for quite a while, I began distributing the software under open-source licenses on GitHub and BitBucket.
